US Politics Session AI Pick
America under the microscope
What: 2026 marks 250 years since the Declaration of Independence. Hay has a strong US politics thread — sharp analysis of the post-election landscape, culture wars, global implications, and what comes next.

Why: The US shapes everything — markets, technology, geopolitics. Worth understanding from people who've spent decades studying it.

Bear Grylls — My Life in Books Booked
with podcaster Jamie Laing
Who: The world's most famous adventurer. Former SAS reservist, youngest Brit to summit Everest at 23, Chief Scout. A genuinely extraordinary life — from a freefall parachuting accident that broke his back to crossing the North Atlantic in an inflatable boat.

What: Part of Hay's "My Life in Books" series. Bear shares the books that shaped him. Expect to be surprised by what an action man reads — and inspired by how stories fuelled his adventures.

Technology, Women & Africa AI Pick
How tech is reshaping a continent
What: How technology is transforming Africa — particularly through the lens of women leading that change. Innovation, mobile banking revolutions, and the leapfrogging of Western infrastructure.

Why: A refreshing counterpoint to Western-centric AI conversations. Real stories of real impact.

Kate & Sophie Raworth — Doughnut Economics Booked
BBC News presenter interviews her economist sister
Who: Kate Raworth — Oxford economist whose Doughnut Economics model has been adopted by Amsterdam, Copenhagen, and dozens of cities. Her TED talk has millions of views. Reimagines economics to meet human needs without overshooting planetary boundaries. Sophie Raworth — one of the BBC's most recognisable newsreaders.

What: Sister interviews sister about one of the most important economic ideas of the 21st century. The family dynamic adds warmth and honesty to what could be a dry policy conversation.

Climate Fiction Prize AI Pick
Stories that imagine our future
What: Climate fiction ("cli-fi") — one of the most vital literary genres of the decade. This session celebrates writing that imagines how we'll live with — and respond to — climate change. Samantha Harvey (Booker Prize winner for Orbital) is at the festival.

Why: A perfect way to close the festival — forward-looking, creative, hopeful. Take the ideas home with you.

Practical Notes

Base: Lower Court, Clifford — ~10 mins drive from the festival site
Between events: Allow 15 minutes between venues on site
Free site access: Dairy Meadows is free to enter — tickets only for individual events
Bookshops: Dozens of independents in Hay town — leave time to explore
Wild Garden: New for 2026 — a reflective space on site between sessions
MUBI Cinema: Free short film screenings — great for filling a gap
Hay Castle: Guided tours at 11am & 2pm daily. 800 years of history, Wye Valley views
Wildcard times: Marked TBC — check closer to the date and book early
Food & drink: Garden Kitchen on site — demos, tastings, great food
